✦ The Journey ✦

Your Complete Route

Tap any stop to explore what awaits — every milestone a new story in the mountains.

🚉 Starting Point

Sarsawa, Saharanpur

Your journey begins in this quiet Uttar Pradesh town. Pack light, check your gear, and board the overnight train to Pathankot. The mountains are calling — and so begins the expedition.

🏠
🚂

🚂 Train — Night Journey

Pathankot

Gateway to the Himalayas. Pathankot marks your first breath of mountain air. Board the legendary Kangra Valley Toy Train here — a narrow-gauge heritage ride through emerald valleys and steep river gorges.

🚂 Toy Train — Heritage Ride

Baijnath Paprola

A 12th-century Shiva temple stands guard over this ancient town. The Baijnath temple is one of the finest examples of Nagara-style architecture in the Himalayas — sacred, serene, unforgettable.

🛕
🪂

🚌 Bus — Short Ride

Bir Billing

The paragliding capital of India and Asia's highest landing zone. Watch pilots launch from Billing at 2400m and soar over pine forests. Camp at Bir village, explore Tibetan monasteries, and eat momos at sunset.

🥾 Trek — Off the Map

Rajgunda Valley

One of Himachal's best-kept secrets — a lush meadow valley hidden above Bir at 2600m. The 12km trek takes you through dense oak and rhododendron forests, across mountain streams to an ancient shepherd settlement.

🏕
🍵

🚌 Bus — Valley Road

Palampur

The tea capital of the Northwest Himalayas. Rolling emerald tea estates stretch before a wall of snow-capped Dhauladhar peaks. Wander through the gardens, taste fresh Kangra tea, and breathe deep.

🚂 Toy Train → 🚌 Bus

Kangra → McLeodganj

Board the toy train one last time for the historic ride to Kangra. Then ascend to McLeodganj — Little Lhasa — where Tibetan culture, Buddhist temples, and mountain energy converge in one of India's most unique towns.

🏔
🥾

🥾 Trek — Day 8

Triund Ridge

The crown jewel of Dhauladhar trekking. 9km to 2875m, where the ridge falls away on both sides and the entire Kangra Valley stretches below. Watch the sunrise paint the Dhauladhar wall pink and gold.

🥾 Multi-Day Trek

Kareri Village & Lake

The grand finale. Trek from Kareri Village through pine forests and moraines to reach Kareri Lake at 2934m — an alpine jewel with crystal-clear water reflecting the surrounding peaks. Camp under stars so bright they feel within reach.

💧
🏁

🚌 Bus → 🚂 Train

Amb Andaura → Sarsawa

The gentle descent. Catch a bus to Amb Andaura railway station and board the train home. 15 days, 3 treks, 1 heritage train, countless memories — your Himalayan story is written.

✦ Unmissable Experiences ✦

Journey Highlights

Eight iconic stops, each a world unto itself — hover to discover what makes each one legendary.

Heritage Railway
Kangra Valley Toy Train
A 164km narrow-gauge journey built in 1929, crossing 993 bridges and 2 tunnels through some of the most dramatic mountain scenery on earth.
Ancient Temple
Baijnath Shiva Temple
Dating to 1204 CE, this Nagara-style marvel is dedicated to Lord Shiva and stands as one of the finest stone temples of the Western Himalayas.
Adventure Sports
Bir Billing
Host of the 2015 Paragliding World Cup. Launch at 2400m and soar for 30+ minutes over pine forests and tea gardens below.
Hidden Valley
Rajgunda Valley
A secret meadow above Bir at 2600m, seldom visited, eternally beautiful — wildflowers, ancient oaks and the solitude of the high Himalayas.
Tea Country
Palampur Tea Gardens
India's second-largest tea growing region, where Kangra tea has been cultivated since 1849. Lush green carpets against snow-capped Dhauladhar.
Tibetan Culture
McLeodganj
Little Lhasa of India — home to the Dalai Lama, Namgyal Monastery, prayer flags, momos, and a culture that survived exile with extraordinary grace.
Iconic Trek
Triund Ridge
Camp on the edge of the Dhauladhar wall at 2875m. Wake to sunrise over snow peaks and a 180° panorama of the Kangra Valley stretching to infinity.
Alpine Lake
Kareri Lake
A glacial lake at 2934m, fed by snowmelt from the Dhauladhar range. Its mirror-calm surface reflects peaks so clearly you can't tell where the mountains end.

✦ Day by Day ✦

The Full Itinerary

Fifteen days, each one different — from slow mornings on moving trains to cold alpine dawns.

Day 01
🚂 Train Journey
Sarsawa → Pathankot

Board the evening express from Sarsawa. Pack snacks, grab a window seat, and watch the plains give way to silhouettes of distant hills as night falls. Arrive Pathankot by morning.

Day 02
🚂 Heritage Toy Train
Toy Train → Baijnath → Bir

Board the legendary Kangra Valley toy train at Pathankot. 164km of mountain magic — tea gardens, river gorges, hilltop stations. Alight at Baijnath for temple visit, then bus to Bir village. Check in to a hostel. Sleep to the sound of prayer bells.

Day 03
🪂 Paragliding Day
Bir Billing — Fly!

Rise at 5AM for the hike to Billing launch site (2400m). Tandem paraglide over pine forests and tea gardens — a 30-minute flight over one of the most spectacular landscapes on earth. Afternoon: explore Tibetan monasteries in Bir. Momos for dinner.

Day 04
🥾 12km Trek • 2600m
Rajgunda Valley Trek

Early morning ascent through dense rhododendron and oak forest to Rajgunda, the "secret meadow" of Bir. Camp overnight beside an ancient shepherd hut with unobstructed views of snow peaks. Stargazing at altitude — unforgettable.

Day 05
🍵 Slow Travel Day
Palampur — Tea Country

Descend from Rajgunda, catch a bus to Palampur. Walk through endless tea estates in the afternoon light, Dhauladhar peaks towering above. Taste fresh Kangra green tea at a local garden. Rest, recharge.

Day 06
🚂 Toy Train → 🚌 Bus
Toy Train → Kangra → McLeodganj

One final toy train ride from Palampur toward Kangra Fort — walk the ancient battlements above the Banganga River. Then ascend by bus to McLeodganj, the Tibetan exile capital. Evening: Namgyal Monastery, Bhagsu waterfall, and a bowl of thukpa.

Day 07
🛕 Exploration Day
McLeodganj Exploration

Full day in Little Lhasa. Morning: Tsuglagkhang Complex (Dalai Lama's temple). Midday: Tibet Museum, Tibetan market, and momo breakfast at a rooftop café. Evening: dal chawal at Nick's, prayer flags at sunset, and live music at a local café.

Day 08
🥾 9km Trek • 2875m
Triund Trek — Summit!

Start at 6AM from McLeodganj. 9km through rhododendron forest and open ridgeline to Triund top (2875m). Camp on the ridge — rock faces of the Dhauladhar wall tower above, the Kangra Valley glows 1500m below. Campfire, chai, clear skies.

Day 09
🌄 Sunrise & Return
Triund Sunrise → Rest Day

Wake at 4:30AM for golden sunrise over the Dhauladhar range — one of the greatest views in India. Descend to McLeodganj by noon. Rest, massage, good food, hot shower. Your legs have earned it.

Day 10
🚌 Bus → Village
Journey to Kareri Village

Bus from Dharamshala to Kareri village (20km). Meet the local families, stay at a basic homestay, help cook dinner. This is where the tourist trail ends — real Himalayan village life begins here.

Day 11
🥾 13km Trek • 2934m
Kareri Lake Trek

The greatest day of the expedition. 13km ascent from Kareri village through forests, moraines, and snowfields to reach the glacial Kareri Lake at 2934m. Camp beside glassy water reflecting Dhauladhar peaks. No phones, no noise, only mountains.

Day 12
🥾 Return Journey
Lake to Village — Return Trek

Wake early, breathe the alpine air one last time. Photograph the dawn reflections on the lake. Descend 13km back to Kareri village, stopping at a dhaba for rajma chawal — the best meal of your life after 2 days on the mountain.

Day 13
🏡 Rest & Reflect
Kareri Village — Recovery Day

Stay another night in the village. Rest sore muscles. Sit in the sun, chat with the village kids, write in your journal. Watch the Dhauladhar peaks glow pink at dusk. These are the moments that stay with you forever.

Day 14
🌿 Slow Travel
Kareri → Amb Andaura

Return to Dharamshala, then take a local bus down through the Kangra Valley to Amb Andaura — a gentle descent through the foothills. Final mountain dinner, pack your bags, look back at the Dhauladhars one last time.

Day 15
🏠 Journey Complete
Amb Andaura → Sarsawa — Home

Board the morning train from Amb Andaura station. Watch the Himalayas grow smaller in the window. You left with a backpack and an open map. You return with stories that will last a lifetime. The mountains will be waiting.
